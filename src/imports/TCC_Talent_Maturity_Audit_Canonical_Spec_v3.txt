Executive + TA Leadership build brief (Figma Make + Web + HubSpot + GA4)
Date: 2026-02-26
1. Executive Summary
This document is the canonical build + UX + measurement spec for the Talent Maturity Audit. It preserves the original high-performing single-flow audit experience (wizard, segmented scoring, local save/restore, and confetti completion moment) while integrating the v2 strategy refinements: AI-first impressions, spend/conversion fragility, and proof-led credibility.
2. Non-Negotiables (Preserve the Initial Structure)
* Single-flow staged wizard (Intro ? Questions ? Submit/Validate ? Results).
* 13 statements; each scored 0 / 1 / 2 via segmented controls (not radio buttons).
* Confetti fires ONLY after a valid submit and score computation; paired microcopy: "Baseline established."
* Local persistence: auto-save each answer to localStorage; restore on reload; explicit "Clear saved answers".
* Results-first: show score + band immediately (no gating before results).
* Email capture occurs AFTER results and is optional (Model A).
3. Information Architecture & URL Recommendation
Recommendation for MVP: keep the audit on the primary domain path to minimize friction and tracking complexity.
Preferred: https://thompsoncollective.co/audit
Why: preserves same-site cookies, simplest GA4 + HubSpot continuity, fewer cross-domain edge cases, and less perceived "handoff" risk for users. A subdomain (e.g., talentaudit.thompsoncollective.co) is viable, but it adds cross-domain tracking setup and introduces an extra trust/latency point.
If you still choose a subdomain, required mitigations:
* Implement GA4 cross-domain measurement (same GA4 property; allow linker between root + subdomain).
* Ensure HubSpot tracking cookie continuity or accept split session attribution.
* Mirror brand header/footer to avoid a perceived context switch.
* Enforce HTTPS + consistent canonical tags; avoid duplicate indexing.
4. Funnel & Measurement Model (MVP)
One funnel. Three meaningful signals.
* Signal 1 - Audit Engagement: CTA2 click from homepage hero or internal links.
* Signal 2 - Audit Completion: Audit submit with computed score + band (confetti moment).
* Signal 3 - High Intent: Calendly session scheduled (or at minimum, Calendly click).

7. The 13 Audit Questions (Canonical Text)
Q1. We have defined 12-18 month hiring priorities tied to business strategy, and we review them quarterly.
Q2. We have identified mission-critical roles (role families) and documented what "qualified" means beyond requirements (skills, traits, dealbreakers).
Q3. We have a documented EVP informed by real employee/candidate insight (not leadership-only).
Q4. Our talent narrative is governed: recruiters and hiring leaders communicate the same core story consistently.
Q5. Our EVP claims are evidence-backed (proof points mapped), and we can defend them across channels.
Q6. We monitor "message-reality drift" (where candidate experience contradicts the promise) and adjust narrative or operations accordingly.
Q7. We have an always-on content engine beyond job postings and publish on a defined cadence.
Q8. Our careers site and priority job content have been updated within 12 months and are structured to convert (clarity, scannability, mobile).
Q9. We actively manage AI-era first impressions: what candidates find in search/AI/review platforms is consistent with our intended narrative (SEO/GEO/AEO basics in place).
Q10. We know which channels drive qualified hires by role family, and we reallocate budget/effort based on performance.
Q11. We have job ad governance (message consistency, inclusive language, quality control) and treat job ads as "claims" that must match EVP.
Q12. We track apply flow friction, candidate communication SLAs, and candidate feedback - and we act on it.
Q13. We have baseline funnel analytics (source quality, conversion, time-to-decision, offer acceptance) and use the data to decide what changes next.
8. Results Output (Band Copy + Priorities + CTA)
Results panel order (keep): Score+Band ? What this means ? 3 priorities ? CTA ? Email capture module.
Primary CTA label (sitewide for this flow): "Book a 30-minute Talent Maturity Audit Discussion"
Band Copy (MVP)
Nascent (0-7)
Hiring is happening, but the system is informal. Outcomes feel inconsistent and hard to diagnose.
Top 3 priorities:
* Establish an EVP-lite: 3-5 defensible claims + proof.
* Fix conversion choke points (job clarity + apply flow) before adding spend.
* Stand up one always-on channel with a weekly publishing cadence.
Developing (8-14)
Pieces exist, but consistency and accountability are weak across channels and leaders.
Top 3 priorities:
* Govern narrative across recruiters + hiring leaders; standardize job ad claims.
* Implement clean baselines by role family (source quality + conversion).
* Turn EVP themes into proof-led assets (specificity beats "culture language").
Aligned (15-20)
You have a coherent foundation; performance is limited by optimization loops and scale discipline.
Top 3 priorities:
* Optimize funnel: apply starts?completes, time-to-decision, offer?accept.
* Scale distribution: coordinate paid/owned/earned to reduce channel waste.
* Tighten AI/search/review footprint to reduce narrative drift.
Optimized (21-26)
Strong governance + measurement. The opportunity is scale, resilience, and defensibility.
Top 3 priorities:
* Expand role-family segmentation (message + channel strategy).
* Operationalize narrative defense (monitoring + response playbook).
* Run a quarterly improvement cadence with benchmarks and decisions.
9. Post-Results Email Capture (Model A - Locked)
Goal: preserve the 'free tool' feel while allowing users to send results to themselves. Do not gate results.
Placement: directly beneath the results panel; do not interrupt confetti moment.
Fields: Email only.
Suggested microcopy:
Headline: "Want a copy of your results + recommended next steps?"
Helper: "No spam. This is just your score and what to do next."
Button: "Send my results"
Success: "Sent. Check your inbox."
10. Notes for Web Designer (UI/UX)
* Maintain the existing audit visual language: editorial, calm, minimal; avoid "quiz app" styling.
* Keep the wizard compact: one primary column, generous whitespace, short line length.
* Segmented controls must be obvious and tappable on mobile (min 44px height).
* Provide persistent progress indicator: "Question 4 of 13" + subtle bar (no heavy gamification).
* Validation should be calm: if unanswered questions remain, highlight them inline and scroll to first missing.
* Confetti moment: lightweight and brief; keep as celebration of completion, not novelty.
* Results panel: prioritize scannability-Band label, then 3 priorities in bullets, then CTA.
* Email capture module: visually secondary (under results) but not hidden; keep one field only.
* Accessibility: ensure contrast, focus states, keyboard navigation for segmented controls, and reduced motion support.
11. Notes for Developer (Implementation)
* Route: /audit (same domain). Ensure homepage CTA2 links to this route (already in HomePage TSX).
* State model: answers array length 13; values 0/1/2; compute score on submit.
* Persistence: localStorage key 'tcc_audit_v3'; save on every change; restore on mount; provide clear/reset.
* Validation: require all answered before submit; track missing indices; focus/scroll to first missing.
* Events: fire talent_maturity_audit_submitted on successful submit with params audit_score + maturity_band.
* Email capture: post-results; call HubSpot form/API or backend endpoint; also fire audit_email_capture event.
* Calendly: track click reliably; scheduling event optional unless confirmed via redirect/webhook.
* SEO: noindex optional while testing; remove at launch; ensure canonical and OpenGraph tags.
* Performance: avoid large animation bundles; confetti library should be lightweight and lazy-loaded.
* Privacy: confirm that audit data stored locally is minimal; disclose via brief note if needed.
